Figure 1. Information-partition framework for antecedent-state monitoring value. Panel a defines the tested increment as the score change obtained by adding antecedent state A to rainfall R and streamflow persistence P for a high-flow target Yq. Panel b summarizes the three competing interpretations: memory partition, storage ceiling and the benchmark null. Panel c retains evidence categories as support or boundary evidence. Panel d states the inference boundaries. The figure frames the study as a persistence-conditioned monitoring problem rather than a generic prediction-performance comparison.

Figure 2. Extended event-definition surface with block-bootstrap uncertainty in the primary MERRA2-GloFAS layer. Panel b compares raw-state, residual-state and rain+persistence-benchmarked surfaces. Panel c reports high-flow event counts and valid bootstrap draws, showing support thinning toward the deepest tail. Panel d reports the share of positive bootstrap draws for the rain+persistence benchmark. The figure supports upper-tail strengthening beyond Q90 rather than a physical threshold mechanism.
